About the Program

Alaska's Only Dedicated Scout High Adventure Base

Alaska Scouting Adventures operates the Chilkoot High Adventure Base in Haines, Alaska, offering custom high-adventure programs for Scouts BSA and youth groups across the United States.

What We Do
Multi-day expeditions in backpacking, sea kayaking, mountaineering, and whitewater rafting — led by professional wilderness guides in one of Alaska's most rugged coastal landscapes.
What You Gain
Teamwork · Resilience · Technical outdoor skills · Backcountry leadership · Expedition planning · Navigation · Glacier travel · Paddling techniques · Team problem-solving

Each expedition is custom-designed to match your unit's goals, experience level, and leadership objectives. Programs are structured, safety-focused, and built around progressive skill development.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Do I need prior outdoor experience?
    No. Programs are approachable with preparation. Guides teach all skills on-site. Scouts should be comfortable hiking with a pack and participating in full-day outdoor activities.
  • What's the minimum age?
    12 years old. Youth should be physically prepared, mentally mature, and capable of managing personal gear in a remote environment.
  • What about bad weather?
    Programs operate rain or shine — this is Southeast Alaska, after all! Guides adjust itineraries as needed for safety. Severe weather may result in schedule changes.
  • How remote are the programs?
    Very remote. No road access. Limited communication. Participants must be prepared for rugged, true wilderness conditions. That's what makes it special.
  • Can adult leaders participate?
    Yes! Adult leaders are welcome and play an important role. All participants must meet physical and program readiness requirements.
  • How is safety managed?
    Conservative guide-to-participant ratios. Emergency communication devices carried at all times. Guides hold Wilderness First Responder certifications and technical credentials.
  • What should participants bring?
    Personal clothing suitable for layering, rain gear, sturdy footwear, sleeping gear, and personal items. A detailed packing list is provided after registration.
  • What is the cancellation policy?
    Deposits and cancellation terms are outlined in the registration materials. Trip protection insurance is strongly recommended.
